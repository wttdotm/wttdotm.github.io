First Light

Installation, Envelopes, Paper, Website
Python, HTML, CSS, and JavaScript
2024-Present

First Light project that gives people the opportunity to be the first person in the world to see a specific star. At the current scale of astronomical knowledge (1.8 billion+ celestial objects), most newly-discovered stars are found by computerized telescopes, processed for their quantifiable characteristics, and stored in huge databases to be later fed into models by researchers. Because these tens of thousands of new stars discovered every day are (a) barely incremental (b) uninteresting individually and (c) visually boring, the vast majority of them will never actually be seen, only calculated with. The story of modern starlight is one where photons originate trillions and trillions of miles away, spend countless millennia getting to our solar system, and end up as numbers in a row of data instead rather than a glint in someone's eye.

I use a series of scripts to scrape astronomy research databases, find the newest unremarkable and academically ignorable stars, and cross-reference them with literature archives to ensure that they have never been covered in any research.

First Light: Discovery

Multimedia Installation
2026

An installation at the San Francisco Exploratorium for their Summer 2026 exhibition "Life in Space." Visitors enter a custom First Light booth and walk through a series of interactive screens where they learn about the scientific process behind First Light, culminating in the opportunity to print out their very own First Light star then and there.

First Light: Envelopes

Envelopes, Paper, Website
2024 - Present

I print out large stacks of the original pictures of these stars upside down and - without looking at them - fold them up and seal them. The result is First Light, a collection of envelopes that let the opener be the first witness to the star, and through the act of looking at it fasten it as real against the cosmos. You can buy one yourself at FirstLightStars.com.

First Light: Emergence

Python, HTML, CSS, and JavaScript
2025

A project currently on display on the online gallery External Pages and visible in the iframe above. First Light: Emergence uses Python scripts to request and fetch the coordinates of disregarded and unseen stars from web-based sky atlases such as Gaia, SIMBAD, and VizieR. It then collects the images of that portion of sky found in three sky surveys - DSS2, SDSS9, and PanSTARRS - each more recent and showcasing the star in greater detail than the last. Every hour, these surveys slowly fade into each other, channel by channel. This careful, unhurried passage from raw color band data to full visual representation does not reject the biases inherited by computer-generated imagery, such as sensor noise and resolution limits: here those artifacts are vital reminders of the images' origins as aftereffects of data collection. After all, with each passing second, the always-improving image on the screen becomes the best picture of that star that anybody has ever seen. The embracing of imperfection and inherent slowness of First Light is crucial to the project, rejecting instant gratification and encouraging viewers to dwell on the question of what constitutes an image and how we decide what is worth seeing.
