Bluesky Firehose

Live public social-media activity

Bluesky's public AT Protocol makes its stream of new posts and replies available in real time. Firesky is a viewer that republishes this flow as it happens: a succession of ordinary updates, reactions, links, jokes, fragments of news, and conversations across languages. It can be filtered by keywords, account, linked domain, language, and post type.

It is not an archive arranged around importance. It is a moving cross-section of what people choose to say in public before anyone has decided whether it matters.

Questions to ask

  • What would a weather report for collective attention look like if it refreshed every second?
  • Can a post become a portrait of the exact moment it was written, even when its author is anonymous or unknown?
  • What changes when the internet is treated not as a library of messages but as an uncatchable live performance?
  • Which sentences recur in different places without ever becoming a conversation?
  • Could you make a slow artwork from a dataset designed to overwhelm any attempt at close reading?
