iDigBio

Digitized natural-history specimen records

iDigBio is a search portal for digitized records from natural-history collections. It brings together specimens, observations, images, collection records, taxonomy, collection dates, collectors, localities, geology, and map coordinates. A biological body becomes a record with a field number, an institution code, an uncertainty radius, and sometimes a photograph.

The database makes it possible to search across collections that were gathered over generations. It also exposes the apparatus of looking: who collected what, where it was found, what was considered worth preserving, and which parts of a living world can be made to fit a form.

Questions to ask

  • What would you look like as a specimen record?
  • Find your favorite animal, what do you see?
  • What is the emotional distance between a creature in the field and that creature as a barcode, coordinate, and cabinet number?
