MNIST is a dataset of 70,000 grayscale images of handwritten digits: 60,000 training examples and 10,000 test examples. Each image is reduced to a 28 × 28 pixel grid and labeled with the number it is understood to depict. It was assembled from digitized handwritten forms and normalized into a benchmark designed to make handwriting legible to machines.

For decades, MNIST has been a standard introductory problem in machine learning. Its digits are anonymous, cropped, centered, and simplified until they become a small controlled universe: a human gesture translated into pixels, a label, and a prediction score.
