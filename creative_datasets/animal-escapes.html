Animal Escapes

Smithsonian National Zoological Park investigations, 2014–2021

This released Smithsonian record, published by GovernmentAttic, lists animal escape and containment incidents at the National Zoo between 2014 and 2021. It includes dates, species, circumstances, response language, search procedures, recovery outcomes, and small details that make an institution's effort to control animal movement unexpectedly visible.

The incidents range from birds slipping through wires to a bison outside its enclosure, a tenrec that could not be located, and the bobcat Ollie, who remained missing overnight. The reports turn the ordinary vocabulary of administrative risk management into a record of improvised encounters between animals, staff, architecture, visitors, nets, gates, and radio codes.

Questions to ask

  • Could an escape report be staged as a workplace drama in which the animal is the only character who never speaks?
  • What does an institution reveal about itself in the instant its boundaries fail?
  • How would the story change if every incident were narrated from the escaped animal's scale: one gap, one hallway, one unfamiliar smell at a time?
  • Could “Code Green” become a score for sound, movement, or collective choreography?
  • Which animals appear to be escaping, and which are simply moving through a world designed to insist that they cannot?
